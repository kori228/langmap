2026-05-28 — Community correction: Lhasa Tibetan tone + "house" definition refinement
  • Lhasa Tibetan (bo) — cat tone correction (commit 6dd5f0b): ཞི་མི /ɕimi˥/ → /ɕimi˩˧/. The written initial ཞ /ʑ/ is a historically voiced fricative; in the Lhasa tonogenesis the voiced series devoices in onset (/ʑ/ → /ɕ/) but the resulting syllable retains the low-register tone, parallel to ཟ za /sa˩˧/ "eat" already in the same row. The earlier `˥` was self-inconsistent with the project's own stated rule (voiced initials → low tone).
  • Contributor: Rzmlvlk (@rzmlvlk). ありがとうございます！
  • house definition softened (this commit): the prior definition strictly excluded both "home" as abstract and "building" as technical, but Wu Chinese 屋里 (oʔ-li, literally "in-the-house") is genuinely lexicalized as the everyday word for one's dwelling — a real cross-dialect pattern, not a translation error. Definition now reads "the natural everyday word for one's residence; in some varieties this surfaces as a locative compound (e.g. 'in-the-house') that has lexicalized as 'house/home'." Updated across all 22 UI languages.
