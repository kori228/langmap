# Hanmap data review #18 — 'Phags-pa, Yuan Zhongyuan-Yinyun & Dungan specialist

## Reviewer self-introduction (ペルソナ自己紹介)

元代音韻史と、その現存する西北方言の末裔である東干語（Dungan）を専門とする立場から本レビューを書く。柱となるのは三層である。第一に **'Phags-pa（パスパ字）正書法**：八思巴文字の各字母と中古音声母の対応、とりわけ影母 ʔ-（ꡝ/ꡗ 字）と疑母 ŋ-（ꡃ 字）の弁別、入声韻尾の表記、全濁清化の反映を Coblin, *A Handbook of ʼPhags-pa Chinese* (2007) に照らして検証する。第二に **『中原音韻』(1324) の韻類体系と入派三聲**：清・濁声母別の入声再配当（全清・次清入→上聲、全濁入→陽平、次濁入→去聲）と、声調コントゥアの内部整合性を Hashimoto の中原音韻研究および寧繼福の校本に基づき確認する。第三に **東干語（甘粛・陝西系官話）**：本データはキリル文字正書法をラテン翻字した surface 行と IPA 行を持つが、その声調（三声体系 I/II/III）、捲舌音の喪失（知章莊→歯音/口蓋音）、-n / -ŋ 韻尾の合流、児化韻 ɚ を Rimsky-Korsakoff Dyer の Dungan studies に照らして点検する。本プロジェクトの `HAN_DATA` モデル（文字ごとに `surface`/`ipa` の二行、register コードで keying）には精通しており、ここでは自分の担当 register（`zh_phagspa` / `zh_yuan` / `dng`）のみを、レビュー #1・#9・#10 が既に扱った論点を重複させずに検証する。

**Reviewer perspective:** Yuan-era phonology and its living Dungan offshoot (Phags-pa orthography → IPA, 中原音韻 rhyme/tone classes, Gansu-Mandarin Dungan)
**Coverage:** `zh_phagspa` / `zh_yuan` / `dng` — all 61 characters sampled; entering-tone set (一七八十日月六木血肉食立北足目) audited exhaustively against 入派三聲; Dungan tone-to-contour and Cyrillic-romanisation-to-IPA mappings audited across all 61 entries.
**Notation conventions observed in the file:**

- `zh_yuan`: digit tone marks 1/2/3/4 mapping cleanly onto IPA contours **1=˥ (陰平), 2=˧˥ (陽平), 3=˨˩˦ (上聲), 4=˥˩ (去聲)** — verified machine-consistent across all 61 chars.
- `zh_phagspa`: romanised Phags-pa surface + IPA; 影母 glottal written sometimes `ʔ` (一/月/魚) and sometimes the modifier-letter `ʼ` (五/央/飲) — the file is mid-migration from `ʼ`→`ʔ`.
- `dng`: superscript tone digits 1/2/3 → contours **1=˦, 2=˨˦, 3=˥˩** (machine-verified, with two stray exceptions flagged below); Cyrillic-romanisation key: `zh`=ʐ/tʂ, `sh`=ʂ~ɕ, `j`=tɕ, `ch`=tɕʰ, `-n`=both /n/ and /ŋ/.

## Issues found

### 1. 足 — `zh_yuan` (surface/ipa) — 全清入 wrongly redistributed to 陽平, not 上聲
- Current: `"tsu2"` (surface), `"tsu˧˥"` (IPA) — i.e. tone 2 = 陽平
- Expected: `"tsu3"` / `"tsu˨˩˦"` (上聲)
- Why: 足 has 中古 initial 精母 (*ts-*, 全清, voiceless unaspirated). The 中原音韻 入派三聲 rule sends **全清入 → 上聲**; **陽平 is the slot reserved for 全濁入** (e.g. 十 禪母, 食 船母 — both correctly tone 2 in this file). A voiceless 全清 initial can never feed 陽平 in any analysis of ZYYY (Hashimoto; 寧繼福 校本). This is the single indefensible tone error in the `zh_yuan` entering-tone set: the file's own 十 `ʂï2`/食 `ʂï2` (全濁→陽平) and 日/月/六/木/肉/立/目 (次濁→去) are all correct, which makes 足→陽平 stand out as a sport.

### 2. 八 — `zh_yuan` (surface/ipa) — 全清入 follows modern Beijing 陰平 instead of ZYYY 上聲
- Current: `"pa1"` (surface), `"pa˥"` (IPA) — tone 1 = 陰平
- Expected (per ZYYY): `"pa3"` / `"pa˨˩˦"` (上聲)
- Why: 八 is 幫母 (*p-*, 全清). 中原音韻 全清入 → 上聲. The value `˥` (陰平) is the **modern Beijing** reflex (bā), not the ZYYY-prescribed one. For a register explicitly labelled Yuan / 中原音韻 this is an anachronism. Sibling evidence: 北 (issue 3) shows the identical 全清→陰平 substitution, confirming the pattern is systematic Beijing-leakage rather than a one-off.

### 3. 北 — `zh_yuan` (surface/ipa) — same 全清入→陰平 Beijing leakage
- Current: `"pei1"` (surface), `"pəi˥"` (IPA) — tone 1 = 陰平
- Expected (per ZYYY): `"pei3"` / `"pəi˨˩˦"` (上聲)
- Why: 北 is 幫母 (全清) 入聲 (德韻). By 入派三聲 it joins 上聲, exactly like 八 and 足. The bare `˥` again reproduces modern Beijing běi. (Coblin 2007 reconstructs the Phags-pa shape of 北 as `bay`/paj — the file's `zh_phagspa` is right; only the `zh_yuan` tone is Beijing-anachronistic.)

### 4. 行:2 — `zh_phagspa` (surface/ipa) — impossible cluster `xɦ`; voiced ɦ violates 全濁清化
- Current: `"xɦaŋ"` (surface), `"xɦaŋ"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"xaŋ"` (or, if a voiced reflex is wanted, `"ɣaŋ"`) — a single fricative, not `x`+`ɦ`
- Why: 行 (行 háng reading) is 匣母 (全濁 voiced velar/glottal fricative ɣ-/ɦ-) 平聲. By the Yuan period 全濁清化 was complete in 中原音韻; 匣母平 had devoiced and merged with 曉母 x-. The file's own `zh_yuan` row correctly gives voiceless `xaŋ˧˥` (陽平). The Phags-pa row `xɦaŋ` is wrong twice over: (a) `x`+`ɦ` is a phonetically impossible voiceless+voiced fricative cluster that occurs nowhere else in the dataset (it is the only `ɦ` in any `zh_phagspa` cell), and (b) the voiced `ɦ` contradicts the devoicing that `zh_yuan` itself encodes. Coblin (2007) renders 匣母 in Phags-pa with the ꡜ/ꡅ letter as a single segment; surface and IPA should read `xaŋ`.

### 5. 五 — `zh_phagspa` (surface) — stray `ʼ` modifier letter where the file elsewhere standardised to `ʔ`
- Current: `"ʼu"` (surface), `"ʔu"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"ʔu"` (surface), to match the IPA and the file's standardised entries
- Why: A consistency, not a phonology, finding (the 疑母→ʔ question for 五 was raised in review #1; not repeated here). The file has migrated 一 (`ʔi`/`ʔi`), 月 (`ʔɥe`/`ʔɥe`) and 魚 (`ʔy`/`ʔy`) to a unified IPA glottal `ʔ` in **both** rows. 五 (along with 央, 飲 below) was left behind with the old Tibetan-transliteration modifier letter `ʼ` (U+02BC) in surface while IPA already uses `ʔ`. Harmonise surface → `ʔu`.

### 6. 央 — `zh_phagspa` (surface) — stray `ʼ` modifier letter (also `y` vs `j` glide mismatch)
- Current: `"ʼyaŋ"` (surface), `"ʔjaŋ"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"ʔjaŋ"` (surface)
- Why: 央 is genuinely 影母 (ʔ-), so the glottal stop is phonologically correct — the only problem is notation. Two surface/IPA mismatches: (a) surface `ʼ` vs IPA `ʔ` (same migration issue as 五/飲), and (b) surface glide `y` vs IPA `j`. The IPA `ʔjaŋ` is the target; surface should read `ʔjaŋ` (or `ʔyaŋ` if the y-romanisation is intentional, but then `ʼ` must still become `ʔ`).

### 7. 飲 — `zh_phagspa` (surface) — stray `ʼ` modifier letter
- Current: `"ʼim"` (surface), `"ʔim"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"ʔim"` (surface)
- Why: 飲 is 影母 (ʔ-), glottal correct; the residual `ʼ` in surface is the last member of the un-migrated trio 五/央/飲. Aligning it to `ʔim` completes the 影母 glottal standardisation that 一/月/魚 already follow.

### 8. 牛 — `zh_phagspa` (surface/ipa) — surface drops the Grade-III medial /j/ present in the IPA
- Current: `"ŋiw"` (surface), `"ŋjiw"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"ŋjiw"` (surface), to match the IPA
- Why: 牛 is 尤韻 三等 (Grade III) 疑母. The palatal medial /j/ is etymologically present and the IPA row records it (`ŋjiw`); the surface row `ŋiw` omits it. (Phonological note: the file correctly keeps 疑母 ŋ- here, unlike its ʔ- treatment of 五/魚 — see issue 16.) Surface should carry the medial: `ŋjiw`.

### 9. 二 / 耳 — `zh_phagspa` (surface) — 日母 written `ri` while sibling 日 writes `ʐɨ`
- Current: 二 `"ri"`/`"ʐɨ"`; 耳 `"ri"`/`"ʐɨ"`; but 日 `"ʐɨ"`/`"ʐɨ"`
- Expected: 二 surface `"ʐɨ"`, 耳 surface `"ʐɨ"` — to match both their own IPA and the sibling 日
- Why: 二, 耳, 日 are the three 日母 止攝 syllables in the set and share an identical Phags-pa shape and IPA (`ʐɨ`). 日's surface was already migrated to `ʐɨ`; 二 and 耳 still carry the older convenience romanisation `ri` (which misleadingly implies a trill + high front /i/). Coblin (2007 §12) transcribes this 日母 class uniformly; the three surface cells should be identical (`ʐɨ`).

### 10. 中:2 — `dng` (ipa) — tone-III contour `˧` is anomalous; all other 去聲 Dungan entries are `˥˩`
- Current: `"jun³"` (surface), `"tɕuŋ˧"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"tɕuŋ˥˩"` (IPA)
- Why: 中:2 is the 去聲 reading (zhòng), correctly tagged surface tone `³`. But Dungan tone III is realised `˥˩` throughout this dataset (15 of the 16 tone-3 entries), e.g. 二 `ɤr˥˩`, 日 `ʐɚ˥˩`, 肉 `ʐou˥˩`, 立 `tʂan˥˩`, 下 `ɕja˥˩`. 中:2 alone has the mid-level `˧`, which corresponds to no Dungan tone category. Machine audit: tone `³` → `˥˩`×15, `˧`×1 (this entry). Correct the IPA contour to `˥˩`.

### 11. 立 — `dng` (surface) — romanisation initial `j` (=/tɕ/) contradicts IPA `tʂ` (retroflex)
- Current: `"jan³"` (surface), `"tʂan˥˩"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"zhan³"` (surface)
- Why: This entry is the Dungan word 站 (zhàn, "to stand", IPA `tʂan`). By the file's own Cyrillic-romanisation key, `j` = /tɕ/ (九 `jyu`=tɕju, 見 `jyon`=tɕjɛn, 中 `jun`=tɕuŋ), whereas the retroflex/ʐ-series IPA `tʂ`~`ʐ` is romanised `zh` (日 `zhyr`=ʐɚ, 肉 `zhu`=ʐou, 人 `zhyn`=ʐəŋ). Surface `jan` therefore mis-encodes IPA `tʂan`. Either the surface should be `zhan` (if `tʂ` IPA is kept) or — since Dungan famously **lost the retroflex series** (Rimsky-Korsakoff Dyer: 知章莊 → dental/palatal) — the IPA should arguably be `tsan`. At minimum the two rows must agree on the series; right now they don't.

### 12. 行:2 — `dng` (surface) — lone `-ŋ` spelling where 14 sibling syllables romanise velar nasal as `-n`
- Current: `"haŋ²"` (surface), `"haŋ˨˦"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"han²"` (surface), to match the file's consistent Dungan orthography
- Why: Dungan's Cyrillic orthography (and this file's romanisation of it) writes the velar nasal coda as plain `-n`: 龍 `lun`/luŋ, 中 `jun`/tɕuŋ, 東 `dun`/tuŋ, 人 `zhyn`/ʐəŋ, 央 `yan`/jaŋ, 羊 `yan`/jaŋ. 行:2 `haŋ` is the **only** surface cell that writes `ŋ` explicitly, breaking the convention (the IPA `haŋ` is fine). Normalise surface to `han`.

### 13. 二 / 耳 — `dng` (ipa) — rhotic written `ɤr` (vowel + separate r) where sibling 日 uses the rhotacised `ɚ`
- Current: 二 `"er³"`/`"ɤr˥˩"`; 耳 `"erdo¹"`/`"ɤr˨˦.tɔ"`; but 日 `"zhyr³"`/`"ʐɚ˥˩"`
- Expected: `"ɚ˥˩"` (二), `"ɚ˨˦.tɔ"` (耳) — or a single consistent rhotic symbol across all three
- Why: 二, 耳, 日 are the 日母 止攝 er化/rhotic syllables. The Dungan reflex is a single rhotacised vowel [ɚ]/[əɻ] (Rimsky-Korsakoff Dyer), and the file's 日 row records exactly that (`ʐɚ`). But 二 and 耳 are transcribed `ɤr` — a back-unrounded vowel `ɤ` plus a stray `r` (ambiguous between alveolar trill and rhotic). This both mis-describes the vowel quality (`ɤ` ≠ rhotic schwa) and is internally inconsistent with the sibling 日. Use `ɚ`.

### 14. 目 / 耳 — `dng` (surface) — disyllabic compounds tagged tone `¹` while their IPA carries the rising tone `˨˦` (=tone II)
- Current: 目 `"yanjing¹"`/`"jan˨˦.tɕiŋ"`; 耳 `"erdo¹"`/`"ɤr˨˦.tɔ"`
- Expected: surface tone digit `²` (目 `yanjing²`, 耳 `erdo²`)
- Why: These are the disyllabic body-part words 眼睛 (yǎnjīng) and 耳朵 (ěrduo). The tone-bearing first syllable is 眼 / 耳, both 上聲 → Dungan tone **II** (rising `˨˦`), and the IPA correctly shows `jan˨˦` / `ɤr˨˦`. The surface superscript `¹` (tone I = `˦`) contradicts its own IPA. Machine audit: surface tone `¹` → IPA `˦`×15 vs `˨˦`×2 — the two `˨˦` outliers are precisely these two compounds. Surface should read `²`.

### 15. 五 — `dng` (surface) — romanised `w` for a syllabic /u/ nucleus, inconsistent with the file's `u`
- Current: `"w²"` (surface), `"u˨˦"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"u²"` (surface)
- Why: 五 in Dungan is a zero-initial syllabic /u/ (IPA `u`, correctly tagged tone II `˨˦` — the expected 疑母 上聲 → Dungan II). The surface `w` (consonantal glide letter) mis-renders a vowel that the IPA writes as plain `u`. Other zero-initial /u/-type nuclei in the file are not spelled `w`. Use `u`.

### 16. 魚 — `zh_phagspa` (surface/ipa) — 疑母 rendered ʔ-, inconsistent with 牛 (疑母) kept as ŋ-
- Current: `"ʔy"` (surface), `"ʔy"` (IPA)
- Expected: flag for consistency — either `ŋy` (matching 牛) or an explicit data note on the 疑母 split
- Why: This is a *systematic-consistency* flag, distinct from review #1's character-specific 五 note. The dataset treats 中古 疑母 (ŋ-) two different ways in Phags-pa: 牛 keeps `ŋjiw` (issue 8), but 魚 (and 五) collapse to glottal `ʔ`. In Coblin (2007) the 疑母 → zero/ʔ merger before high front rounded 撮口 vowels is real, but it is a *conditioned* split, and the file applies it without comment, producing an apparent contradiction (牛 ŋ vs 魚 ʔ, both 疑母). Either reconstruct 魚 with the ŋ- the file gives 牛, or add a data note documenting the 撮口-conditioned 疑母→ʔ merger so the inconsistency is principled rather than accidental.

### 17. 四 — `zh_phagspa` (ipa) — apical-vowel nucleus written `z̩` where the file's own convention is `ɿ`
- Current: `"sɿ"` (surface), `"sz̩"` (IPA)
- Expected: `"sɿ"` (IPA), to match the surface and the `zh_yuan` convention
- Why: 四 has the apical (空韻) high vowel after a dental sibilant. The Phags-pa surface row uses the sinological apical-vowel symbol `ɿ` (`sɿ`), and the **`zh_yuan` IPA for the very same 四** also uses `sɿ˥˩`. But the `zh_phagspa` IPA row uniquely writes it as syllabic `z̩` (`sz̩`), which conflates the apical *vowel* with a syllabic *consonant* and clashes with the file's established `ɿ` notation. Use `sɿ` in the Phags-pa IPA row for consistency.

## Summary of systematic issues

1. **`zh_yuan` 入派三聲 leaks modern Beijing tones for the 清入 class.** The 次濁入→去聲 and 全濁入→陽平 rules are applied flawlessly (日/月/六/木/肉/立/目/目 → 去; 十/食 → 陽平). But the 全清/次清入 characters (八, 北, and especially 足) carry modern Beijing reflexes instead of the ZYYY-prescribed 上聲 — with 足→陽平 being phonologically impossible for a 全清 initial. A register labelled 中原音韻 should follow 入派三聲, not Beijing (issues 1–3).
2. **`zh_phagspa` 影母 glottal notation is mid-migration.** 一/月/魚 use unified `ʔ` in both rows; 五/央/飲 were left with the legacy Tibetan-transliteration `ʼ` in surface. Finish the `ʼ`→`ʔ` migration (issues 5–7).
3. **`zh_phagspa` surface rows lag their IPA rows.** 牛 (medial /j/), 二/耳 (日母 `ri`→`ʐɨ`), 央 (`y`/`j`), and 四 (`z̩`/`ɿ`) all show surface/IPA divergences where the IPA is the more correct/standard form (issues 8, 9, 6, 17).
4. **`zh_phagspa` 全濁清化 violated once:** 行:2 `xɦaŋ` retains a voiced ɦ (and an impossible x+ɦ cluster) that the `zh_yuan` row has correctly devoiced to `xaŋ` (issue 4).
5. **Dungan surface↔IPA encoding drift.** Tone-digit/contour outliers (中:2 `˧`; 目/耳 disyllables tagged `¹` not `²`), retroflex-series romanisation (立 `j`≠`tʂ`), velar-nasal spelling (行:2 `haŋ` alone uses `ŋ`), rhotic symbol split (二/耳 `ɤr` vs 日 `ɚ`), and a stray glide-for-vowel spelling (五 `w`). Most are mechanical normalisations against the file's own established conventions (issues 10–15).
